Safe Travels

Georgian Travel Tips

Language & Communication

  • Learn basic Georgian phrases: Gamarjoba (Hello), Madloba (Thank you)
  • Russian is widely spoken by older generation
  • English is common in tourist areas and among youth
  • Download offline translator app before traveling

Money Matters

  • Currency: Georgian Lari (â‚¾) - 1 USD â‰ˆ 2.7 GEL
  • ATMs widely available - Bank of Georgia and TBC have English
  • Cash preferred in small shops and restaurants
  • Tipping: 10% in restaurants, round up for taxis
  • Exchange money at banks or official exchanges, not street

Transportation

  • Marshrutkas (minibuses) are cheapest for intercity travel
  • Bolt and Yandex taxi apps work well in cities
  • Metro in Tbilisi costs 1â‚¾ - buy Metromoney card
  • Agree on taxi price before starting journey if no meter
  • Georgian Bus app for comfortable intercity buses

Food & Drink

  • Must try: Khachapuri, Khinkali, Churchkhela, Matsoni
  • Tap water is safe to drink in major cities
  • Wine is central to culture - toasting is important
  • Vegetarians: Many dishes contain meat, ask specifically
  • Eat khinkali with hands - never with fork and knife!

Connectivity

  • Buy SIM card at airport - Magti or Geocell recommended
  • Tourist SIM packages available for 20-30â‚¾
  • Free WiFi common in cafes and restaurants
  • 4G coverage good in cities, patchy in mountains

Culture & Etiquette

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches - cover shoulders
  • Women need head covering in some churches
  • Georgians are very hospitable - accept offered food/drink
  • Remove shoes when entering homes
  • Avoid political discussions about Russia or territories

Health & Safety

  • Georgia is very safe for tourists
  • Pharmacies (apteka) are everywhere - many speak English
  • EU/US medications widely available
  • Emergency numbers: 112 (all services)
  • Travel insurance recommended for mountain activities

Shopping

  • Dry Bridge Market in Tbilisi for antiques and Soviet items
  • Wine, churchkhela, and spices make great souvenirs
  • Carrefour and Goodwill for groceries
  • Most shops open 10am-8pm, closed Sundays in small towns
  • Bargaining expected at markets, not in shops
