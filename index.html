Georgia Travel Guide

Everything You Need to Know

Language & Communication
Basic Phrases

• Gamarjoba - Hello
• Madloba - Thank you
• Nakhvamdis - Goodbye
• Russian widely spoken by older generation
• English common in tourist areas
• Download offline translator app

Essential
Payment Methods
Money & Cards

Currency:
• Georgian Lari (₾/GEL)
• 1 USD ≈ 2.7 GEL
• 1 EUR ≈ 3.0 GEL

Cards Accepted:
• Visa/Mastercard everywhere
• Contactless payments common
• Small shops prefer cash

ATMs:
• Bank of Georgia (orange)
• TBC Bank (blue)
• Both have English interface
• Max withdrawal: 2000-3000₾

Tips:
• Exchange at banks/official exchanges
• Keep small bills for marshrutkas
• 10% tip in restaurants

Banking Money
Transportation
Getting Around

• Marshrutkas: Cheap minibuses for intercity
• Bolt & Yandex taxi apps work well
• Tbilisi Metro: 1₾ with Metromoney card
• Agree taxi price before journey
• Georgian Bus app for comfortable buses
• Trains available for major routes

Transport Budget
Food & Drink
Must Try Dishes

• Khachapuri - Cheese-filled bread
• Khinkali - Dumplings (eat with hands!)
• Churchkhela - Walnut candy
• Matsoni - Yogurt
• Wine is central to culture
• Tap water safe in cities
• Vegetarians: Ask specifically, many dishes have meat

Culture & Etiquette
Local Customs

• Dress modestly in churches
• Women need head covering in some churches
• Georgians are very hospitable
• Accept offered food/drink
• Remove shoes in homes
• Avoid political discussions
• Toasting is important at meals

Culture Respect
Health & Safety
Emergency Info

• Georgia is very safe for tourists
• Emergency: 112 (all services)
• Pharmacies (apteka) everywhere
• EU/US medications available
• Travel insurance recommended
• Mountain weather unpredictable
• Bring warm clothes for Kazbegi
